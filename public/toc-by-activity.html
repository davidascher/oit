mozilla Open Innovation Toolkit

Tools for each phase

Specific content grouped by key activities; Gather insights + Ideate + Prototype and Test

Gather insights

  • Get out of the building: How to interview and recruit
  • Cover your basics: Interviews
  • Get your basic done fast: Guerilla Research
  • Understand their day or tacit moments: Mobile moments
  • Uncover the real story: Contextual Inquiry
  • Never ship without: Usability Testing with people
  • Ensure it’s usable: Usability Heuristic testing
  • Test the solution early and fast: Prototype testing
  • I’m not an MBA: Quick Competitive Review
  • Still not an MBA: Quick Market Research
  • Our guiding principle! Understand your Job-to-be-done

Ideate

  • High-octane team workshop to define the product: Product Design Sprint
  • Co-create with real users: Participatory Design Session
  • Fun and accessible way to align thinking: System Thinking / Visual Collaboration Workshop
  • Fast way to generate new ideas: Brainstorm session
  • Fast way to improve current ideas: Feature Ideation / User Need Workshop
  • Framing the question well is most of the battle: How might we...?
  • Thinking in experiences, not transactions: Storyboarding
  • Everything is connected! Systems Diagram / Mindmap

Prototype and Test

  • Simple making to try out your ideas: Prototyping 101
  • Making sense of user needs in a few steps: Visual prototyping / storyboarding / user flow
  • Fast and dirty interactions to test assumptions: Paper prototype
  • A little more “life-like” to show users: Interactive prototype / Click-through (walled garden)
  • Our Mozilla definition of this milestone: MVP
  • Evaluating your product with real people: User testing
  • Early product concept testing based on description alone: Prototype testing
  • Method to unpack assumptions: 5 Whys
  • Visual method to surface priorities and needs: Card Sort
