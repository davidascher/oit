MVP

Objective
Rapidly create and test the smallest feature set of a product in order to demonstrate that its value proposition resonates with real users.
Outcome
Demonstration of viability, feasibility, desirability

Time

6-9 months depending on complexity of the product

Number of people needed

4-6 team members, typically 4 engineers, and a product lead and UX designer

Equipment/materials/space

The usual

Detailed steps

  • The most important aspect of the MVP is understanding that you will accrue technical debt and design debt. This “startup” mindset is almost in direct opposition to a large sw org mindset around building product.
  • Minimal + viable = what is the minimum you can do to still show value.
  • Distill your product down to its simplest form (where it still retains value). Ask questions like: can I provide this value without building it?
  • Use off the shelf technology where possible.
  • Isolate new-to-the-world engineering, and only build if the mockup or prototype overwhelmingly required it
  • Continue reducing scope and simplifying the product as you build it out -- focusing on the unique value of the product, not the technical achievement.
  • Continuously test with real users and refine

Degree of Difficulty

High
